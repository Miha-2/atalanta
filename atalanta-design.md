# Atalanta BC — Design Reference

> Research-based visual identity guide for design work at Atalanta Bergamasca Calcio.
> Sources: official club website (atalanta.it), Wikipedia, kit manufacturers, brand color registries, and observer analyses of their social/marketing output.

---

## 1. Brand Essence

Atalanta BC are nicknamed **La Dea** (The Goddess) — a direct reference to Atalanta of Greek mythology: a swift, fierce, virgin huntress. Every design choice should be filtered through this mythological identity. Speed, elegance, and relentless aggression are the emotional registers to hit.

The club is deeply rooted in **Bergamo**, a city in the Lombard foothills. Provincial pride, working-class grit, and underdog-turned-European-champion is the story arc that fuels the brand's tone: ambitious but never arrogant, historic but modern.

Key brand phrases used in official communications:
- **"La Maglia Sudata Sempre"** — "The Shirt Always Sweated" (inside collar motto since Joma era, carried forward culturally)
- **"Liberi di Sognare"** — "Free to Dream" (used as 2024/25 Champions League slogan on stadium signage)
- **"La Dea"** — used in headlines, slogans, and fan culture

## 2. Colour Palette

### Primary Colours

| Colour | Role | HEX | RGB | CMYK | Pantone |
|--------|------|-----|-----|------|---------|
| Nerazzurri Blue | Primary / dominant | `#0d68b1` | 13, 104, 177 | 93, 41, 0, 31 | PMS 7455 XGC |
| Black | Co-primary | `#000000` | 0, 0, 0 | 0, 0, 0, 100 | Process Black C |

Note: the official blue sits between a medium azure and a royal blue — never navy, never sky. When in doubt reference the vertical stripes on the home kit: a clear, strong mid-tone blue.

### Secondary / Accent Colours

| Colour | Role | HEX |
|--------|------|-----|
| White | Background, typography on dark | `#ffffff` |
| Gold / Amber | Crest border, heritage accent | `#c9a84c` (approx.) |

Gold is a heritage colour from the crest's circular border — use sparingly and only in premium/heritage contexts (trophies, anniversaries, formal print).

### Usage Principles
- Blue and black should appear together in roughly equal weight — neither dominates. The famous vertical stripes are the reference.
- White is the primary text colour on dark backgrounds. Never use off-white or cream.
- Avoid gradients. The brand is flat and bold.
- On digital: use `#0d68b1` for the blue. On print/physical: verify against PMS 7455 XGC.

## 3. The Crest

### Structure (current crest, since 1993)
- **Shape:** Ellipse / oval
- **Centre:** Profile silhouette of Atalanta's head and flowing hair — the hair flows in black-and-white stripes (the original colours before the 1920 merger)
- **Background:** Vertically split — blue left, black right (mirroring the kit stripes)
- **Typography:** "ATALANTA" in bold uppercase across the top of the ellipse; "1907" at the base
- **Border:** Gold/amber double ring around the central circle, then outer ellipse in blue/black

### Mythological iconography
The profile is the absolute core icon of the brand. It is the goddess herself — rendered as a clean silhouette, not a detailed illustration. This head profile is the equivalent of a wordmark for this club. Do not decorate, recolour, or morph it outside of official contexts.

### Logo versions to know
- **Primary:** Full ellipse with name, year, and crest profile
- **Profile-only:** Just the goddess head in a circle — used for app icons, watermarks
- **Wordmark alone:** "ATALANTA" or "ATALANTA BC" — used on some digital banners

## 4. Typography

No single public typeface is officially documented. Observed across the website, kits, and marketing materials:

### Headline / Display
- Heavy, condensed sans-serif
- All caps almost universally
- High tracking (wide letter spacing) in secondary headlines
- Characteristics: bold weight, geometric or slightly humanist, strong vertical strokes
- Safe approximations: **Bebas Neue Pro**, **Barlow Condensed Bold**, **Industry**, or **Montserrat ExtraBold Condensed**

### Body / UI
- Clean, modern sans-serif at regular weight
- The official website uses a neutral grotesque (similar to Inter, Source Sans, or Roboto families)

### Tone of use
- Headlines are short, punchy, all-caps
- Numbers / stats treated large and bold (scores, stats graphics)
- Italian and English copy appear side by side on the official site — layouts must accommodate both

## 5. Visual Language & Aesthetic

### Photography
- **Action-first:** Players photographed mid-movement, in-game. Never static product-style poses unless it's kit launch.
- **High contrast:** Blue and black kit photographs well against white stadium seats or night-game darkness. Embrace that contrast.
- **Bergamo as backdrop:** The Città Alta (Upper City), the stadium, the hills — location imagery ties the club to territory.
- **Fan culture:** Curva Nord tifo imagery (ultras choreography, blue/black banners and flares) is a major visual asset.

### Graphics / Social Media Style
- Colour blocks: heavy use of solid blue and black panels with white type over
- Diagonal lines and splits referencing the shirt stripes
- Large, bold numbers — matchday countdowns, scorelines, stats
- Circular / oval framing of player faces (echoing the crest shape)
- The goddess profile used as a watermark or texture element in backgrounds
- "Wallpaper Wednesday" is an established content series — high-production matchday wallpapers are a signature output

### Motion / Video
- Fast-cut highlight packages
- Club docs use atmospheric Bergamo footage (cobblestoned streets, misty hills)
- European competition campaigns get cinematic treatment — dark, epic tone

## 6. Kit Identity

The kit is inseparable from the visual identity:
- **Home:** Vertical blue and black stripes — equal-width, alternating. Has been this way since 1920. Currently manufactured by **New Balance** (from 2025-26, replacing Joma).
- **Away:** Typically white with blue/black accents. Variable season to season.
- The **"La Maglia Sudata Sempre"** inner-collar inscription is a notable detail — translates to "The shirt always sweated." A battle-cry, not a slogan.

---

## 7. Language & Tone

- **Italian by default**, with English for international communications
- **Passionate but measured** — not bombastic. The club communicates with pride, not hype.
- **Mythological register** encouraged: "La Dea," "Nerazzurri," "The Goddess" — lean into the mythology in premium comms
- **Bergamo identity** is a feature, not a limitation — "Orobic" (from Orobie, the local mountains), "Bergamasco" culture should be referenced in place/community content

## 8. Must-Have Visual References

These are the non-negotiables for any Atalanta design work. Get clean, official versions of these before starting:

### Primary Brand Assets
1. **The Crest** — full ellipse version (SVG/AI); also the profile-only version for smaller applications
2. **Blue & Black vertical stripe pattern** — as a repeating tile (exact spacing from the 2025-26 New Balance kit)
3. **"ATALANTA" wordmark** — standalone, white on dark and dark on white

### Iconic Photography
4. **Europa League Final — May 22, 2024 (Dublin, Aviva Stadium)** — Atalanta 3–0 Bayer Leverkusen. The defining moment of the club's modern era. Lookman hat-trick, squad celebrating, trophy lift. This imagery is everywhere.
5. **Curva Nord tifo / ultra choreography** — the blue and black crowded stand is a powerful brand image
6. **New Balance Arena (formerly Gewiss Stadium)** — exterior and interior, particularly the renovated 2024 full-capacity view
7. **Città Alta, Bergamo skyline** — the medieval upper city with stadium/hills — key for territory-rooted design

### Kit References
8. **2024-25 Joma home kit** (final Joma season — historically significant)
9. **2025-26 New Balance home kit** (new era — first New Balance kit)

### Historical / Heritage
10. **1963 crest** (first appearance of the running goddess — useful for anniversary and heritage content)
11. **1984 circular crest** (the iconic head-only version beloved by ultras culture)

## 9. Things to Avoid

- Do not use the blue as navy or indigo — it must read as a medium azure
- Do not use the crest on coloured backgrounds without checking contrast (the gold border disappears on yellow/light backgrounds)
- Avoid generic "football clichés" (ball patterns, boot silhouettes) — Atalanta's identity is about the goddess, the stripes, and Bergamo
- Do not soften the brand — this is not a pastel, rounded-corner club. It is sharp, proud, and direct.
- Avoid large amounts of white space in a "premium minimalist" manner — Atalanta skews bold and full

---

## 10. Competitive Positioning Reference

For context in pitching direction: Atalanta's brand sits between:
- **The romanticism** of traditional Italian football clubs (mythology, history, crest iconography)
- **The modernity** of high-performance European football (data-driven play, Champions League presence, New Balance partnership)

They are not Juventus (corporate, monolithic black/white). They are not a Napoli (maximalist, tourist-brand). They are proudly Bergamasca — specific, intense, and on the rise. Design should reflect ambition earned, not inherited.
